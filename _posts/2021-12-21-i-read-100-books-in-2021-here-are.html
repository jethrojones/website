So my top five books are as follows:

5. The Parasitic Mind: How Infectious Ideas Are Killing Common Sense

What I really liked about this book is the reliance on a method for proving and disproving things.

I think the author is missing a lot by only believing in the scientific method as a way to prove things and not accepting other ways of knowing, but for the purpose for which he wrote the book, it makes sense.

4 The Reincarnationist Papers

This is actually a fiction book, but it really made me think. What would I do if I my spirit were to enter a different body and I could remember my previous lives? Would I be completely hedonistic like the characters in this book, or would I do something really good to leave my mark on the world?

The movie Infinite is actually better than this book. Also, another movie that was better than the book is Old.

3 Humor Seriously

This book teaches how to not be so serious at work. Definitely worth a read. I imagine it has to be hard to write a book about being funny, and actually be funny. Footnotes were dynamite, actually worth reading.

2 How to Take Smart Notes

This book really changed how I take notes, and it is powerful. It has made writing books so much easier. I'm still not a pro at it, but I can already see how powerful it is for me.
