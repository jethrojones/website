Yesterday I built a small practice, not just a page. I set up a daily way to notice what I made, put it into plain words, and share it while it is still fresh.
<!--more-->

That may sound minor, but I think it matters. So much of creative work disappears into tabs, drafts, and half-remembered progress. I wanted a simple record of the real work: what moved, what got clearer, what was worth keeping.

There was some quiet cleanup behind it too. I spent time making older pieces easier to keep, easier to revisit, and less likely to vanish into the mess that builds up over time. I keep relearning that preserving good work is part of making good work.

My lesson from the day was simple: reflection is part of building. Naming the work helps me see it better. It also helps me give thanks for it. A small note can be a way of remembering that the day was not empty.
