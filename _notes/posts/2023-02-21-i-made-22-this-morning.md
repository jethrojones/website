Sometimes you get in a situation where your options have lost value or
gained a lot of value and you need to do something. That’s what I did
last week.

If you remember from last week, I had sold calls on SOFI. The price of
SOFI was pretty volatile last week, and then it dropped a lot at the end
of the week. I’ll get into the details in a minute!

Rolling options is a strategy used by options traders to extend or
**adjust the terms** of an existing options contract. This involves
closing out an existing options position and simultaneously opening a
new position with similar but different terms.

You can’t open a new position on a different stock, that’s not rolling.

Rolling means you adjust the:

- expiration date

- strike price

- or both

In this situation I did both. Details below.
