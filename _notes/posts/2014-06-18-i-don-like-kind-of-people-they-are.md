I was talking to a student today about what kinds of things she thought
needed to happen in her school with the new year coming up. 

<div>

  

</div>

<div>

She said something so insightful, that I could not believe that it was a
7th grader saying it to a bunch of adults. She elaborated on a concept
that many teachers do not yet grasp. The clarity and simplicity with
which she came to her conclusions was astounding. 

</div>

<div>

  

</div>

<div>

She said (a little paraphrasing from my memory), "We need to do
something about the discipline at our school. When I see what these kids
who have to go into ISS (in-school suspension) become…well I dot like
the kind of people they are becoming. Going to ISS doesn't help them do
anything different and that makes me sad."

</div>

<div>

  

</div>

<div>

I asked her what she thought should happen and she said, "I don't know
what the best thing to do is, but I know ISS is not working for the kids
at my school, because it doesn't make them change anything." 

</div>

<div>

  

</div>

<div>

We were then able to have a discussion about what we, as adults, can do
to help kids who make mistakes learn how to make better decisions. This
girl was a little sheepish to say these things to a bunch of educated
adults, but she was right on the money in her assessment of the
situation. 

</div>

<div>

  

</div>

<div>

I admire this girl for saying what she believed. I admire her for being
perceptive and aware of the situation. 

</div>

<div>

  

</div>

<div>

Have a Good Life. 

</div>
