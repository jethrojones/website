Yesterday I spent time tending my own corner of the internet. I worked through a stack of older notes, pages, and media so they would be easier to find, easier to read, and less likely to get lost as the web keeps changing.
<!--more-->

Most of the work was quiet. I cleaned up rough edges, brought older pieces into better shape, and made the site feel more whole. It was less about making something brand new and more about making sure past work still has a living home.

That mattered to me more than I expected. I’ve made too many useful things over the years to let them drift away just because they were built in a different season. Care is part of building too.

One lesson from the day: maintenance is creative work. It may not look impressive from the outside, but it protects what matters. I’m grateful for that reminder. Sometimes faithfulness looks like repair.
