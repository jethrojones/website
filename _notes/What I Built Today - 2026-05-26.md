Yesterday I worked on the unglamorous part of building: making things clearer for the people who have to trust and use them. A website got simpler and more honest. A support flow got easier to hand off. An export got closer to something a real person could actually use.<!--more-->

None of that sounds dramatic, but it matters. Good work is often just removing friction. When someone lands on a page, asks for help, or needs a report, they should not have to guess what happens next.

I like that AI can help with this kind of work. Not just making new things fast, but helping me notice the rough edges I would normally leave for later. Yesterday felt like a day of sanding things down so they serve people better.

My takeaway: clarity is a form of kindness. It saves time, lowers anxiety, and makes the next step easier to take.
