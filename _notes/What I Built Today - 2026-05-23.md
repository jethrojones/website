Yesterday I spent time preparing my website for a careful move. The work was not flashy. I was cleaning old pages, fixing broken paths, and making sure good work from years ago would still be easy to find in its next home.
<!--more-->

A lot of creative work gets buried under time. Old posts lose their shape. Helpful pages drift. Videos get harder to watch. I wanted the site to feel less like an attic and more like a living library.

What mattered most was continuity. I do not want a new front door to mean lost memories or dead ends. If someone comes looking for something useful I made years ago, I want them to arrive instead of giving up.

My lesson from the day was simple: moving forward is better when you honor what came before. Preservation is not separate from creation. It is part of it. I am grateful for the chance to keep tending the garden instead of starting over.
