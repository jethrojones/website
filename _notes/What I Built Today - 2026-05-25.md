I spent part of today making the first steps of signing up feel simpler and less fragile.
<!--more-->

Instead of asking people to do everything at once, I broke the setup into three smaller steps. First, choose the name. Then add the person the updates are for. Then add the person back home who will manage it.

That change matters because people are usually willing to take one clear next step. They often stall when the whole process shows up at once. I wanted the path to feel more like a conversation and less like paperwork.

I also cleaned up one of those hidden weak spots that only shows itself when something goes wrong. If the final handoff has a hiccup, the work that already succeeded should still count. That is not flashy, but it makes the whole thing feel more trustworthy.

Small lesson from today: when a system feels heavy, the answer is often not more explaining. It is a better sequence.
